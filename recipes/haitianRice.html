Haitian Rice

Haitian rice is a staple in any Haitian household. Most haitian meals are not complete without a side of rice. Haitian rice can come in many varieties and can also be made with a variety of beans. Learn below how to make this delicious meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of rice
  • Optional 1 cup of dry beans (pinto, red, or black beans)
  • 8 cups of water
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablspoon of butter
  • 1 large diced onion
  • 1 teaspoon of salt or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves or 1/4 teaspoon of ground gloves
  • 3 cubes of chicken bouillon
  • 1 whole scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme (2 sprigs)
  • 1 cup of coconut milk

Steps

  1. Add water, beans, salt, 1 tablespoon of olive oil to large pot and let beans cook for 1 hour on medium high heat until beans are soft
  2. Once the beans are cooked, strain the water for use later
  3. In seperate large pot add 1 tablespoon of olive oil and saute the onions and garlic for about 2 minutes
  4. Add the coconut milk, chicken bouillon, beans and mix thoroughly
  5. Add cloves and 6 cups of the bean water and bring to boil
  6. Mix the rice and whoel scotch bonnet pepper and allow rice to cook for about 20 minutes or until there is little water left
  7. Reduce the heat to medium,add thyme, butter, and cover the pot with a lid and allow the remaining water to be absorbed. About 10-15 min.
  8. Once cooked fluff the rice and serve.
